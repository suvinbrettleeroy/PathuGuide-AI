{% if language == 'ta' %}வேலை பங்கு விளக்கம்{% else %}Job Role Explanation{% endif %}

What is a Deputy Collector?

A Deputy Collector is a senior administrative officer responsible for managing revenue administration, land records, disaster management, and overall development of a taluk or sub-division within a district.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Land revenue collection and management
  • Maintaining land records and mutation
  • Implementation of government welfare schemes
  • Disaster management and relief operations
  • Election duties as Assistant Returning Officer
  • Law and order support in the taluk
  • Grievance redressal and public welfare
Career Growth:
  • Entry: Deputy Collector / SDM (Sub-Divisional Magistrate)
  • Mid-Level: Additional Collector / Joint Commissioner
  • Senior: District Collector / Commissioner
  • Top-Level: Principal Secretary / Chief Secretary
Why this suits you: Ideal for candidates with strong administrative skills, public service motivation, and ability to handle diverse responsibilities.

{% if language == 'ta' %}விண்ணப்ப செயல்முறை{% else %}Application Process{% endif %}

Step 1: Watch for Notification

TNPSC releases notifications on official website: www.tnpsc.gov.in

  • Check website weekly
  • Subscribe to TNPSC email alerts
  • Follow TNPSC official Twitter account
  • Notification usually in December/January
Step 2: Check Eligibility
  • Age: 21-32 years (relaxation for reserved categories)
  • Education: Any degree from recognized university
  • Nationality: Indian citizen
  • Physical Fitness: Medically fit
Step 3: Online Application

Apply through TNPSC online portal within deadline (usually 30 days)

  • Register on TNPSC website
  • Fill personal, educational, and other details
  • Upload photo, signature, certificates (scanned copies)
  • Pay application fee (₹150 for general, exemption for reserved categories)
  • Payment via Net Banking/Debit Card/Credit Card
  • Take printout of application for future reference
Step 4: Download Hall Ticket

Hall ticket available 10-15 days before exam on TNPSC website

  • Login with registration number
  • Download and print hall ticket
  • Verify all details (name, photo, exam center)
  • Carry hall ticket + photo ID to exam
Step 5: Exam Stages
  1. Preliminary Exam: 200 MCQs, 3 hours, General Studies
  2. Main Exam: For qualified candidates, 200 MCQs, Aptitude + General Studies
  3. Interview: Personality test (100 marks)
  4. Physical Measurement: (If applicable for certain posts)
Step 6: Results
  • Preliminary Result: 2-3 months after exam
  • Main Result: 3-4 months after main exam
  • Final Result: After interview (merit list)
  • Check results on TNPSC website with registration number
Step 7: Document Verification

Selected candidates called for certificate verification

  • 10th, 12th, Degree certificates
  • Community certificate (if applicable)
  • Transfer certificate
  • Conduct certificate
  • Aadhar card, PAN card
Step 8: Appointment & Training
  • Appointment letter issued by respective department
  • Training period: 1-2 years (depends on post)
  • Probation period: 2 years
  • Posting based on rank and availability
